Why the feature numbers don't add up to the blended total — and why that's intentional

If you add up the "extra deals" across every feature row below, you'll get a bigger number than the blended estimate at the bottom. That's not a rounding error. It's the most important thing to understand about how this works.

Follow one deal through the system — this is Mike
1
Instant response + after-hours
Mike submits a lead at 9pm Saturday. Matador responds in seconds. Without it, Mike hears nothing until Monday — and he's already texting two other stores by Sunday morning.
2
Follow-up sequences
Mike goes quiet for a few days. The sequence sends a timely, relevant follow-up on day 3. He re-engages. His rep gets notified with the full conversation thread.
3
AI tag-in safety net
Mike replies at noon Tuesday. His rep is with a customer on the lot. The AI steps in, holds the conversation, and keeps the thread warm until the rep is free 20 minutes later.
4
Appointment booking + reminders
Mike books a visit. A confirmation goes out. A reminder fires the morning of. Mike shows up. His rep closes him. One deal — four features that each contributed to getting him there.
That's one deal — but four features touched it on the way to the close. If you stacked the feature rows and totaled them, you'd count Mike four times. A GM will catch that immediately and your credibility takes a hit.

The blended estimate is the honest number. It reflects total incremental deals, accounting for the fact that most won deals will have been supported by multiple features working together. Think of it like a football team: the quarterback, the receiver, and the offensive line all contributed to the touchdown. It's still one touchdown — not three.
